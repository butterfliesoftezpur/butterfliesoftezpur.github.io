The slow flight is characteristic of this butterfly. Status, Distribution and Habitat: The Psyche lives in shaded places in gardens, secondary scrub derived from deciduous, semi-evergreen and evergreen forests and also in deciduous and semi-evergreen forests. Usually it keeps close to the undergrowth. It is most common from early monsoon to early winter, occurring in other seasons too, although in smaller numbers. It is common all over peninsular India. Its global distribution extends over the entire Oriental region.

  • Habits

    • :

    In keeping with its delicate appearance, the Psyche's flight is extremely weak, fluttering and always close to the ground. When disturbed it flies erratically,but not faster. If caught it draws its legs close to the body, lies on the side and pretends to be dead. Its flight is sustained and it takes a long time to settle. Before doing so, it investigates the spot by slowly hovering over it. If disturbed there, it again flies about for a long time searching for a new spot where to rest. Once it settles, the dull underside and greenish striations help to camouflage it. The black spot on the fore wings is its only distinct marking and it is hidden by drawing the fore wings between the hind wings. The butterfly does not show any special basking behaviour. It feeds on small flowers such as Tridax procumbens, Bidens and Alternanthera. It very rarely visits wet soil.

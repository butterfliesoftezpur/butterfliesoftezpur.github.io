  • Common name

    • Common Indian Crow

  • Morphological characters

    • Male: Upperside of wings black or glossy brown; fore wing with a sunmarginal and a marginal row of white spots, anterior spots of both these series sometimes absent, submarginal spots in areas 4 and 5 usually smaller than the others, a short narrow brand in area 1b present. Hind wing with a submarginal row of white spots, the later forming short stripes below vein 5. Underside of both wings usually with a few discal spots and a spot in apex of cell. Female: as in male bur upperside of fore wing without brand in area 1b.

  • Variations

    • This species shows variation in ground colour of wings, size, shape and presence or absence of white spots. The following eight subspecies are found in India. i) E. core core (Cramer)- Upperside of wings broadly paler over the outer areas; fore wing with submarginal and marginal spots prominent, more or less equal, or decreasing in size towards apex, and no basal spot in area 3. Widely distributed in India, Himalaya. a) f. vermiculata Butler- Upperside of fore wing with the spots increasing in size towards apex. Sikkim. ii) E. core godartii Lucas- Upperside of fore wing with a varying violaceous apical patch, submarginal spots small, always a discal spot in area 3, a prominent brand in area 1b; hind wing submarginal and marginal spots almost equal. Manipur. Annam, Southern Myanmar, Thailand, Vietnam (Tonkin). It has following four forms. a) f. layardi Druce- Upperside fore wing apical area without violaceous dusting over the apex. b) f. dominans Fruhstorfer- Upperside fore wing apical ara chiefly white, forming patches. c) f. recussa Fruhstorfer- Upperside of fore wing with grayish white apical area. d) f. defigurata Fruhstorfer- Upperside and underside hind wing without antemarginal dots. iii) E. core andamanensis Atkinson- Upperside pale brown, fore wing with two smaller discal spots closely placed to postdiscal diamond shaped spots in areas 2 and 3. South Andaman Islands. iv) E. core bumila Evans- Much darker than andamanensis; upperside fre wing with discal spots samller, postdiscal and submarginal spots larger. Andaman Islands. v) E. core Ferrari Tyler- Like bumila but paler, upperside fore wing with postdiscal spots and much smaller. South Andaman Islands (North Sentinal Island). vi) E. core scherzeri C. Felder- Upperside dark brown and outwardly cinnamom, a small brand in area 1b in male. Underside with distinct discal spots but without postdiscal and submarginal spots. Car Nicobar. vii) E. core camorta Moore- Very much like scherzeri but outer borders much paler, especially the female; a small brand in male. Central Nicobar Islands. viii) E.core simulatrix Wood- Mason & de Niceville_ Underside fore wing usually with apical spots, the latter appearing on upperside. Male without a brand. Female upperside with borders of both wings very much paler than in male. South Nicobar Islands. Wing expanse: Male,female 85-95 mm. Habits: The butterfly is slow flying. It may be seen sailing lazily along in all types of regions or flying about shrubs and bushes for its foodstuff. It migrates with other Danaines. It is a pest on vegetables. It shows Mullerian mimicry as it is mimicked by Chilasa and the female of Hypolimnas bolina. Habitat and ecology: This is the commonest of the Crows, occouring throughout India below 2450m. It appears from time to time in the low, wet, jungle regions of southern India. It is abundant during the monsoon.

  • Life history

    • The egg is nearly cylindrical for two-thirds its height and anteriorly rather pointed and dome-shaped. It is about 1-4 mm in length and I mm in breadth. The colour at first is white, thereafter turning yellow, then orange and lastly becoming greyish. The larva is dark brown, sometimes nearly violet or with a tinge of claret, transversely banded with white; or bluish-white and banded with brown. It is about 47 mm in length, 6.5 mm in breadth, 6 mm in height and length of tentacles on segment 3 being 10 mm. The pupa is silvery-golden and marked with dirty light brownish. The pupal stage lasts 6-9 days, probably according to the humidity of the air.

  • Food-plants

    • Anodendron paniculatum (Lamtani), Cryptolepis glandiflora (Chawal chatti), C. sinensis, Ficus benghalensis (Banyan), F. glomerata (Counry fig, Goolar), F. indica, F. religiosa, (Pipul), Hemidesmus indicus (Indian Sarsaparilla), Holarrhena antidysenterica (Kura, Kurchi, Dudhi), Holigarna arnottiana, Ichnocarpus frutescens (Kali Dudhi), Nerium oleander (Oleander), Nerium sp., Streblus asper (Siamese Rough Bush).

  • Distribution

    • India: Assam, Northern and Peninsular India, Punjab, West Bengal. Elsewhere: Bangladesh, Indo-'China, Indonesia (Java, Sumatra), Sri Lanka.

  • Status

    • Very common.

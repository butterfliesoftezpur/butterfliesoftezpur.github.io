  • Common name

    • Grey count

  • Field Characters

    • The Grey Count is a dark brown butterfly with a slight bronze-coloured sheen. There are short black lines on the four wings but the most striking characteristic is the pale grey or silvery white margin of the hind wings, which also extends to the lower half of the fore wings. The underside is pale grey with numerous black markings The sexes look alike.

  • Status, Distribution and Habitat

    • This is a butterfly of low and mid-elevation deciduous forests. It is particularly common in riverine patches in the moist deciduous forests and in forest clearings or paths in evergreen forests. It prefers shaded places. It is active throughout the year, but usually uncommon during the monsoon months. It is distributed over the Western Ghats, parts of the Eastern Ghats, then from Kumaon eastwards to southeast Asia.

  • Wingspan

    • 65-80 mm

  • Larval Host Plants

    • The larval host plants of the Grey Count are two large trees that are common in the deciduous forests. They are: Careya arborea and Melastoma malabathricum.

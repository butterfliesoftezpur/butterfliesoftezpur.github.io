  • Common name

    • Spotted Palmfly

    .

  • Characteristics

    • In males, the upper sides are dark brown with iridescent purple, a spot beyond apex of cell, some discal spots and a series of post-discal spots light purplish-blue. Hind wing very dark brown, sometimes with a post-discal series of obscure bluish-white spots. Underside dark chestnut-brown; both wings covered with fine white transverse lines towards the terminal half. Females are similar, but the iridescent purple confined to the apical half of the forewing, the spots almost white. In addition the forewing has a dull white streak along the dorsum and a broader streak in inter-space 1. Hind wing with similar white streaks in the inter-spaces, crossed pre-apically by round black spots, terminal margin dark brown. Underside paler than in the males, more closely covered with the white transverse fine lines. Forewing overspread

  • Distribution

    • It is commonly found in India: Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Meghalayaetc. and Indochina in the Indomalayan realm.

  • Wingspan

    • 40-80mm

  • Food Plants

    • Larvae feed on Trachycarpus fortunei and Musa paradisiaca.

  • Status

    • Common

  • Common name

    • Common small flat

  • Field Characters

  • It is a dull black or brown butterfly with semi transparent spots on the wings. The underside of the wings is grey-brown with diffused dark spots. The male and female look similar.

  • Status, Distribution and Habitat

    • This species is most common at the end of the rainy season, but occurs uncommonly in the post-monsoon months as well. It is common, though usually not abundant, in almost all habitats except arid ones, where the Spotted Small Flat is common. It occurs in openings and edges in evergreen and semi-evergreen forests, in deciduous forests, scrub and short grassland-savannahs. It flies on the plains of the Deccan as well as in the hills, but is more frequently encountered at lower elevations. Except for the very dry northwest India, it occurs throughout the country, in Sri Lanka and in the Indo-Chinese region.

  • Habits

    • This little butterfly flies extraordinarily fast for its size. However, unlike most others of its family, its flight is somewhat erratic and jerky comparatively often. It abruptlylands on the substratum while flying very fast, but lands smoothly. It flies close to the ground and sometimes returns to the same spot for perching. It feeds from flowers of herbs and small bushes. It visits birds' droppings and wet rocks also, but rarely. It always keeps its wings spread. Like the Common Spotted Flat, it goes to rest on the undersides of leaves, but basks on the upper sides with it's wings fully spread.

  • Larval Host Plants

    • Its larval host plants are acanthaceous herbs and small shrubs: Asystasia spp. and Blepharis asperrima. These species grow in neglected places around human habitations, in deciduous forests and openings in evergreen forests.

    • 30-35 mm

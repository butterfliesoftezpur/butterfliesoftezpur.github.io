  • Common name:

  • Common Peacock

  • Diagnostic characters:

  • Upperside black dusted with golden green scales, a green band which may be diffused, faint or obsolete and hind wing with a large blue or green patch.

  • Morphological characters:

  • Male-Uppers ide of fore wing with a golden-green discal band, anteriorly short, narrow and not reachig costa and posteriorly broader and terminating at inner margin, wooly separated scent stripes on veins 1-3 ,and those on veins 2 and 3 being strongly narrowed at both ends; hind wing with a large blue or bluish green patch occupying base of area 4 and outer portions of areas 5-7, posteriorlyusually prolonged into a band areasl-3, a tornal claret red lunule traversed inwardly by an obscure blue line and edged above the lunule by back, three or four claret red submarginal spots may be present. Underside of wings chocolate brown; fore wing with grey stripes between veins forming grey distal area always entering the cell; hind wing with a submarginal series of claret red lunules traversed with inwardly by a purplish blue line and black spots. Female:Like male but larger and much paler on fore wing and without any scent stripes.

  • Variation:

  • The species shows variation in extent of golden-green discal band and long pale discal streaks in fore wing and size and colour of discal patch in hind wing. The species has also wet and dry season forms. There are two subspecies in India: i) Papilio polyctor ganesa Doubleday-The upperside forewing with golden-green discal band absent or if present then rarely reaching apex. The underside of fore wing with long pale discal streaks entering the cell and reaching termen. Sikkim. North Myanmar. It has a following form and an abberant. a) P. polyctor ganesa f. malTytler-Hind wing patch brilliantly green instead of blue. It is cold weather form occuring during February and April. Nagaland. b) P. polyctor ganesa Ab. porphyria Jordan- Underside of hind wing with very enlarged submarginal spots. ii) Papilio polyctor polyctor Boisduval - The upperside forewing with golden-green discal band,prominent and reachingapex. The underside of fore wing with obscure pale discal streaks not entering the cell.Uttarakhand (Kumaon). Pakistan (Chitral). It includes a single form. a) P. polyctor polyctor f. peeroza Moore-It is dry season form and is much smaller. Wing expanse: Male, female 90-130mm.

  • Habits:

  • This is one of the extremely beautiful butterflies of India. The subspecies, ganesa Doubleday got its name after the Indian God “Ganesa”.The butterfly is biovoltine and appears from February to October. The smalldry season form appears before the rains, settling in large numbers on damp patches of mud and the banks of the streams. The larger wet season form is seen in the gardens

  • Habitat and ecology:

  • This butterfly is very common in the warm valleys. It is found in the Himalaya and hills of north-east India from low elevations to 2150m

  • Life history:

    • :

  • The young larva is very dark. The half-grown and full-grown larvae are green with the thorax thickened above like a shield. The pupa is bluish-green, usually with brown markings.

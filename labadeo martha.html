  • Common name

    • Thai Knight

  • Morphology

    • In both sexes, the uppersides are tawny red and the basal area is crossed by fine black lines, the apex of cell by a faint white spot. Both wings crossed by a transverse discal white band followed on the forewing by a series of white lunules and on the hind wing by a series of black lunules and a sub-terminal sinuous fine black line on both wings. Apex of forewing white. Underside: dull brown the base of wings dusted with greenish-white scales; the markings as on upper-side but diffuse, the sub-terminal line margined with white

  • Distribution

    • Lebadea martha is the commonest and most widespread species in the genus, being found in India, Nepal, Myanmar, Thailand, peninsular Malaysia, Sumatra, Sarawak, Brunei and Sabah.

  • Habitats

    • This species is found in forested areas at elevations between sea level and about 600 metres.

  • Wingspan

    • 60-70mm. Food plants: Ixora congesta (Rubiaceae), I. javanica (Rubiaceae)

  • Status

    Common.

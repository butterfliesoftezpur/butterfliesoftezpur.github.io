  • Common name

    • Apefly

  • Diagnostic characters

    • Males are slightly darker towards the apex of the forewing, also a more or less quadrate whitish spot beyond the apex of the cell on the same wing.

  • Morphological characters

    • Male- upperside pale dull brown,slightly darker,in some specimens this spot is slightly diffuse.Underside:pale,silky,brownish white,forewings and hindwings crossed by numerous slender dark brown strigae which are outwardly slenderly edged.Females are slightly paler brown and apex darker in the upperside and underside precisely same as that of the male.

  • Variation

    • The species is very variable and found in the indomalayan realm that belongs to lucaenids or blue family. i) S.e.epeus(India,Sri Lanka to Peninsular Malaya,Nicobars,Mergui and southern Yunnan ii)S.e.dilama(Moore,1878)(Taiwan) iii)S.e.fangola(Kheil,1884)(Sumatra,Nias possibly Borneo) iv)S.e.nubilus(Moore,1884)(Andamans,Pulau Tioman) v)S.e.pharnus Felder,1860(Kai.Buru,Ambon,Halmahera,West irian vi)S.e.semperi Fruhstorfer,1919(southern to central Philippines,Luzon) vii)S.e.strigatus Semper,1889(Southern to Central Philippines) viii)S.e.substrigata(Snellen,1878)(Sulawesi) ix)S.e.titius Fruhstorfer,1919(Java,Bali,Sumba,Sumbawa,possibly Damar and Lombok)

  • Habitat and ecology

    • The butterfly flies rapidly and erratically.Occasionally,it has a habit of flying rapidly around a bush,and landing to rest persistently on its favourite perch.

  • Life history

    • The larva of S.epeus are a potential predator of different species of mealybugs in India.The predator completes its life cycle in 23.8 days with four larval instars.Mean incubation period of egg is 3.9 days.Mean duration of development of larval stages, prepupa and pupa was 9.4,0.9 9.5 days respectively.

  • Status

    • Rare

  • Morphological characters

    • Male: Upperside black,with greenish or bluish sheen; fore wing with or without a short red streak at base, light bluish stripes between the veins, these stripes not reaching outer margin and becoming indistinct towards costa; hind wing with lightbluish stripes between the veins being prominent and extending into apex of cell. Underside of fore wing dull black, with a basal red spot on the cell, stripes between veins more pronounced and entering the, cell; hind wing with 3-5 basal red spots, a postdiscal and a submarginal series of black spotsabove the back centred red spots in areas 1 and 2, respectively, in the tornal area, the postdiscal spots on their inner edges having bluish lunules, submarginal black becoming obscure towards costa. Female:Upperside fore wing with about basal third red in the cell, this red spot touching a large basal blackish spot below the cell; hind wing towards margin and separated by ground colour into a series of submarginal spots, one of these spots bordered by red in area 2, a white stripe alonginner margin having bluish brown stripe followed by black centred red ring distally. Underside of fore wing stripes more prominent between the veins; hind wing with broader stripes and submarginal spots, white area with postdiscal and marginal brown spots, whitearea 2 with postdiscal and submarginal brown spots but marginal spot white Variation:Only this subspecies occurs in India.   • Wing expanse

    • Male, female 120-150 mm.

  • Habits

    • :

    The male visits flowers and its flight is swift and restless, whereas the female is sluggish and sails more slowly. It occurs commonly near the settlements and in open woods. It is a pest on fruits. The tailless females are known to be bred from the green eggs of tailed female.

  • Habitat and ecology

    • :

    It occurs in the northern localities from the spring to the autumn and in the southern localities throughout the year. It is very common at low elevations and may ascend to 2135m in the Himalaya.

  • Life history

    • :

    The young larva is brown anteriorly on the thorax and on each of the last two abdominal segments with a pair of light spines, which disappear later. When full grown, it is dark velvety green, here and there bluish, the thorax swollen, with spectaclemarking and black transverse band. The scent-fork in the young larva bIack, afterwards red. The pupa is green or brown but ,yellow above between the wing-cases.

  • Food-plants

    • :

    Citrus aurantium (Khatta), Citrus spp., Michelia champaca (Champaka), Paramygna scandens.

  • Distribution

    • :

    India: Assam, Sikkim. Elsewhere: Myanmar.

  • Status

    • :

    Not rare.

  • Common name

    • Striped Blue Crow.

  • Diagnostic characters

    • Upperside of wings dark brown, fore wing glossed blue and with white or violet-white spots, hind wing unmarked in male but with white streaks in female.

  • Morphological characters

    • Male- Upperside of wings dark brown; fore wing glossed with brilliant blue and bearing white and violet ... white spots, a spot in apex of cell in, an ,elongated spot a much curved series of five-six postdiscal spots and its costal spot smallest, a sinuous seriesof submarginal spots and a marginal series of spots incomplete apically, an ,elongate violet-white spot at vein 2 betweenpostdiscal and submarginal spots. Hind wing unspotted, apical half overlaid with greyish scales, a small yellow patch of specialized scales present in cell. Underside brown and spots distinct, fore wing with both submarginal and marginal series of spots incomplete apically. Hind wing with discal spots in areas 3 and 5, submarginal series of spots incomplete below vein 4 and marginal series of spots incomplete above vein 5. Female- Upperside lighter brown; fore wing with blue shot area smaller, spots as in male but white and larger, an elongate discal spot below costa; hind wing with narrow white streaks in the cell and in areas beyond cell, a submarginal series of white spots, the latter irregularly arranged anteriorly. Underside mostly as on upperside, spots and streaks distinct on both wings; fore wing with a streak and a conical spot in the cell, the conical spot joined above it to a short, narrow and elongate spot, and another curved streak in area 1b

  • Variation

    • The species exhibits variation in extent of blue gloss, size and shape of spots and streaks. There are following two subspecies found in India: i) E. mulciber mulciber (Cramer)- Upperside of fore wing dark brown glossed with brilliant blue violescent white spots but lighter brown in female. Andaman & Nicobar Islands (Nicobar-Bompoka Island; Andaman Island), Assam (Mishmi HilIs), Himachal Pradesh (Shimla), Nagaland, Uttarakhand (Kumaon, Mussoorie),Punjab (Fategarh). Elsewhere: China, Malaya, Myanmar (Chin Hills), Nepal, Tong-king. ii) ii) E. mulciber kalinga (Doherty)- Upperside of fore wing lesser blue, spots larger and whiter in male and underside of hind wing without streaks in the cell in female. Orissa (Ganjam), Eastern Ghats, Orissa, Rajasthan (Jaipur), Tamil Nadu. Vizagapatnam. Wing expanse: Male, female 90-100 mm.

  • Habits

    • This species flies about 1- 6m above the ground. It is very fond of flowers, where it sits with the wings closed. The females fly lower over bushes and scrubs. The males bask in sunshine and are attracted to dung, urine, salt sources, wetrocks and damp patches. It is mimicked sex for sex by Elymnias mlalas (Palmfly) and Chilasa paradoxa(Great Blue Mime) of the subfamilies Satyrinae and Papilioninae, respectively. The individuals of winter (January) brood are smaller but those of summer (July) brood are larger.

  • Life history

    • :

    The larva is cylindrical, with four pairs of long, fleshy subdoreal filaments which have pink bases and black tips. Its body is pinkish-white with lateral yellowish blotches. The pupa is short and thick across the middle; thorax concave behind; abdominal segmenta dorsally convex,metallic golden-brown with a darker brown anterior stripe.

  • Food-plants

    • Argyreia sp., Ficus sp., lchnocarpus sp., Nerium oleander (Oleander, Rose Bay). Very fond of flowers.

  • Distribution

    • India: Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Assam, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Uttarakhand, Uttar Pradesh (Fategarh), Tamil Nadu, West Bengal. Elsewhere: China, Indonesia (Borneo, Java, Sumatra), Malaya, Myanmar, Taiwan, the Philippine Islands,Vietnam (Tonkin).

  • Status

    • :

    Common in North-east India and rare elsewhere. This species is protected under Schedule IV of the Indian Wildlife

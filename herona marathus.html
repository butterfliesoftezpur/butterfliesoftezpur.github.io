  • Common name

    • Pasha

  • Characteristics

    • The adults are dark brown above with two yellow or white discal bands on forewing and hindwing. They have a basal streak in vein 1b of forewing. Underside of forewing, in vein 5 with white streak in outer half till the termen.

  • Habitat and Distribution

    • It is fairly common up to 4000 ft (1200 m) in the Peninsular Thailand, far north of Malaysia Other subspecies in N. E. India:Assam, Sikkim, Darjeeling rest of Thailand and N. Laos, N. Vietnam and S. China Most frequent between 2000–3000 ft (600–900 m). It is very fond of over-ripe fruits. Visits damp patches for puddling. According to Haribal (1992), when disturbed it sits with head down and wings close to tree trunks and thus perfectly camouflaged.

  • Larval Food plants

    • In India, a Celtis sp (Ulmaceae) has been recorded.

    • 70–90 mm
